=== COURT HIERARCHY IN INDIA ===

India follows a four-tier court hierarchy for civil and criminal matters:

1. SUPREME COURT OF INDIA
   - Highest court in the land; located in New Delhi.
   - Established under Article 124 of the Constitution.
   - Has original, appellate, and advisory jurisdiction.
   - Hears appeals from High Courts; handles constitutional matters.
   - Decisions are binding on all courts in India.
   - Chief Justice of India heads the Supreme Court.

2. HIGH COURTS
   - One or more per state; Article 214 of the Constitution.
   - Hears appeals from Sessions Courts (criminal) and District Courts (civil).
   - Has original jurisdiction in some matters (e.g., writ jurisdiction under Article 226).
   - Supervises all subordinate courts within its jurisdiction.
   - Examples: Bombay High Court, Delhi High Court, Madras High Court.

3. DISTRICT COURTS / SESSIONS COURTS
   - Presided over by District Judge (civil) / Sessions Judge (criminal).
   - District Court is the principal civil court of original jurisdiction per district.
   - Sessions Court handles serious criminal offences (Sessions cases).
   - Hear appeals from Magistrates' Courts and Munsiff Courts.

4. SUBORDINATE / LOWER COURTS
   - Civil side: Munsiff Courts / Civil Judge Courts (Junior Division / Senior Division).
   - Criminal side: Judicial Magistrate Courts (First Class, Second Class).
     - Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM) heads criminal subordinate courts.
     - Metropolitan Magistrates in metropolitan areas.
   - Family Courts handle matrimonial disputes.
   - Labour Courts, Consumer Forums, Lok Adalats are specialised forums.

=== SPECIALISED TRIBUNALS ===

- National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) – corporate insolvency matters.
- Income Tax Appellate Tribunal (ITAT) – tax disputes.
- National Green Tribunal (NGT) – environmental cases.
- Armed Forces Tribunal – disputes related to armed forces.
- Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (District, State, National).
- Fast Track Courts – established for speedy disposal of serious crimes.
- Debt Recovery Tribunals (DRT) – bank debt recovery.

=== JURISDICTION CONCEPTS ===

Original Jurisdiction: The court hears a case for the first time.
Appellate Jurisdiction: The court hears appeals against lower court decisions.
Territorial Jurisdiction: A court can only hear cases within its geographical area.
Pecuniary Jurisdiction: Civil courts have monetary limits on the value of suits they can hear.
  - Munsiff / Junior Civil Judge: suits up to a prescribed limit (varies by state, often ₹1–3 lakh).
  - Senior Civil Judge: up to the district court limit.
  - District Court: unlimited pecuniary jurisdiction.
Subject-matter Jurisdiction: Some courts are limited to specific types of cases.

=== IMPORTANT NOTE ===
All civil matters are governed primarily by the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC).
All criminal matters are governed by the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (CrPC).
