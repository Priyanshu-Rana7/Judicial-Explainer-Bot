DOCUMENT: Court Hearings – What Happens During a Hearing
SOURCE: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC) and Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (CrPC)

=== WHAT IS A COURT HEARING? ===

A court hearing is a formal session where the judge, lawyers, and parties appear before the court to conduct the legal proceedings. Different stages of a case involve different types of hearings.

=== TYPES OF HEARINGS IN A CIVIL CASE ===

1. FIRST HEARING (Return/Admission Hearing)
   - The court examines the plaint for admission.
   - If admitted, the suit is numbered and summons are issued to the defendant.
   - Date is given for the defendant's appearance.

2. APPEARANCE OF DEFENDANT
   - Defendant appears (personally or through advocate).
   - Files Vakalatnama (authority letter for advocate).
   - Is given time to file Written Statement.

3. WRITTEN STATEMENT HEARING
   - Written Statement is filed by the defendant.
   - Plaintiff may file a Replication (reply to written statement).
   - Court may pass interim orders (e.g., ad interim injunction) if urgently needed.

4. FRAMING OF ISSUES HEARING
   - Both parties present pleadings.
   - Court frames issues — specific questions of fact and law to be decided.
   - This is a critical hearing; issues define the scope of the trial.

5. EVIDENCE HEARINGS (Several dates)
   - Plaintiff's side: witnesses appear, file affidavits, are cross-examined.
   - Court records evidence in writing.
   - After all plaintiff witnesses are done, defendant leads evidence.
   - Evidence is concluded with a formal order closing evidence.

6. ARGUMENTS HEARING
   - Advocates for both sides present oral arguments based on evidence and law.
   - Written synopses / notes of arguments may be submitted.

7. JUDGMENT HEARING
   - Court pronounces its judgment on a fixed date.
   - Judge reads the operative part; full judgment may be uploaded later.

=== TYPES OF HEARINGS IN A CRIMINAL CASE ===

1. REMAND HEARING
   - Held immediately after arrest.
   - Police produce the arrested accused before the Magistrate within 24 hours (Article 22, Constitution; Section 57, CrPC).
   - Court decides whether to grant police custody (PC) or judicial custody (JC).
   - Bail application is often made at this stage.

2. BAIL HEARING
   - Accused or their advocate argues for bail.
   - Prosecution opposes bail citing gravity of offence, flight risk, witness tampering risk.
   - Court grants or refuses bail with or without conditions.

3. CHARGESHEET COGNIZANCE HEARING
   - Police file chargesheet; court takes cognizance.
   - Summons or warrant is issued to the accused.

4. CHARGE FRAMING HEARING
   - Court and parties consider whether charges should be framed.
   - Accused may apply for discharge.
   - If charges framed, accused pleads guilty or not guilty.

5. PROSECUTION EVIDENCE HEARINGS
   - Public Prosecutor examines witnesses.
   - Defence cross-examines each prosecution witness.
   - Documentary evidence (exhibits) are formally produced and marked.

6. SECTION 313 STATEMENT HEARING
   - After prosecution evidence, accused is examined by the court.
   - Court puts each incriminating piece of evidence to the accused and records their response.
   - Accused cannot refuse to answer (no oath; answers not treated as evidence against them).

7. DEFENCE EVIDENCE HEARINGS
   - Accused may lead their own witnesses.

8. ARGUMENTS AND JUDGMENT
   - Final arguments by Public Prosecutor and defence counsel.
   - Court pronounces judgment: Acquittal or Conviction.
   - If convicted, separate sentencing hearing may be held.

=== GENERAL HEARING ETIQUETTE ===

- All persons must rise when the judge enters or exits.
- Address the court as "Your Honour" (subordinate courts) or "My Lord / Milord" (High Court / Supreme Court).
- Advocates wear prescribed dress: black coat / gown, white shirt, white bands.
- Mobile phones must be silent or switched off.
- Court proceedings are recorded in the order sheet (Roznama).
- Adjournments: when neither party is ready or a witness is unavailable, the case is adjourned to the next working day.

=== MENTION AND URGENT HEARINGS ===

- A party may "mention" a matter before the court at the beginning of the day's list to draw attention to urgency.
- Courts have an urgent/mentioning roster for urgent injunction applications.

=== ORDER SHEET (ROZNAMA) ===

Every hearing result is recorded on the Order Sheet:
- Date of hearing.
- Who appeared (advocate names).
- What happened (evidence recorded, arguments heard, adjournment granted).
- Next date and purpose of next hearing.
Parties can obtain certified copies of order sheets.
